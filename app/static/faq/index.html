What is AQI?
AQI stands for Air Quality Index. It is a standardised scale used to communicate how polluted the air is. The higher the number, the worse the air quality and the greater the health risk. It ranges from 0 (perfect) to 500+ (hazardous).
What do the AQI levels mean?
AQI Range Category Who is affected
0–50 Good None
51–100 Moderate Unusually sensitive individuals
101–150 Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups People with asthma, heart or lung disease, elderly, children
151–200 Unhealthy Everyone may begin to experience effects
201–300 Very Unhealthy Everyone; serious effects more likely
301+ Hazardous Everyone; emergency health warnings
What is PM2.5, PM1.0, and PM10?
PM stands for Particulate Matter. Tiny particles suspended in the air. The number refers to the maximum size of particle in micrometres (µm). PM2.5 particles are 2.5µm or smaller and are the most dangerous because they can penetrate deep into the lungs. PM1.0 are even finer. PM10 includes larger dust and pollen particles.
Where is the sensor located?
The sensor is located outdoors in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It monitors the immediate local environment and does not represent city-wide air quality.
What sensor hardware is used?
The outdoor sensor is a Sensirion SEN55, which measures PM1.0, PM2.5, PM4.0, PM10, temperature, and humidity. It is connected to an ESP32 C3 Mini microcontroller running ESPHome and reports to Home Assistant over Wi-Fi.
How often does the data update?
The live readings on the main page refresh every 60 seconds. The 24-hour history graphs display data averaged into 5-minute intervals.
How is the AQI calculated?
AQI is derived from the PM2.5 reading using the US EPA formula. A correction factor is also applied to account for humidity, which can cause optical particle counters to overestimate PM2.5 concentrations in humid conditions which is very common in Cambodia.
Why does the AQI sometimes spike suddenly?
Short spikes are usually caused by localised sources such as motorbike exhaust, burning, or cooking smoke passing near the sensor. The 5-minute averaged graphs smooth out these brief events to show broader trends.

Yes, the project is open source and available on GitHub.
